Coding Courses

The Story behind Sparker

I am a self taught software engineer. I taught myself through online courses. But there was a problem. I realized to truely gain the knowledge to build something like make my own minecraft clone from scratch, make a game engine from scratch, or my own website from scratch, it was a mangled mess of random tutorials and courses. And it sucked.

I decided to fix online education, and build the software focused school of the future that would replace college courses and the mangled mess of online learning. I didn't care how long it would take or how hard it would be. It was inevitable someone would do it eventually. So why not me?

The Mangled Mess

Yay! I learned the basics of scripting? What course do I take next?... 3 hours later, you are still searching.

I found there is rarely an answer to that question. To solve it I came up with "pre-requisite" courses. The idea is that any course can set another course as its pre-requisite!

Say someone uploads a "Intro to Scripting" course, then someone else uploads a "Intro to Unity" course. The "Intro to Unity" course can set "Intro to Scripting" as a pre-requisite, so you know which course to take first.

This "hierarchy" system allows natural curriculums and course pathways to evolve as more courses are uploaded! Solving the problem of "What course do I take next?".

Quality of learning Experience

If you've ever taken online college (or any online course) you may notice a problem in the "quality of the experience". It can be hard to contact the teacher and get help, and sometimes you have no idea what he is saying so you just blindly follow along.

Some online courses solve the first problem by having a dedicated discord server, so the teacher and other students and chat live and share their screen. Which is awesome! But I wanted all courses on sparker to have dedicated discord servers.

And well, each course on sparker has its own builtin discord-style chat system! check it out! Code-highlighting is also supported through markdown.

I plan on having (simplified) live voice and video + screensharing (just like in discord) as soon as I can find/pay poeple to help me build it (or figure it out myself).

The second problem of "not getting it" I found could be fixed by teaching courses to in-person focus groups.

I actually spent 6 months testing this course on 5 friends of mine. I ended up throwing the course away because it was fundamentally flawed. And by fundamentally flawed I mean... I rewrote the course 3 times. I am forever indebted to them. And will continue to test courses out with in-person focus groups to make sure our courses are the highest quality on the internet.

Build a Portfolio...

A degree doesn't really show employers what you are capable of. A portfolio of work you've done will. Thats why I decided sparker shouldn't offer certificates as a "proof of work". Instead "proof" comes as showcase portfolio posts (exact number varies per course and is set by the course's author).
