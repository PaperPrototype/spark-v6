Coding Courses

Time to ditch degrees and switch to portfolios.

Founder Abdiel Lopez

I am a self taught software engineer. I taught myself through online courses. But there was a problem. I realized to truely gain the knowledge to build something like make my own minecraft clone from scratch, make a game engine from scratch, or my own website from scratch, it was a mangled mess of random tutorials and courses. And it sucked.

I decided to fix online education, and build the software focused school of the future that would replace college courses and the mangled mess of online learning. I didn't care how long it would take or how hard it would be. It was inevitable someone would do it eventually. Why not me?

The Mangled Mess

Yay! I learned the basics of scripting? What course do I take next?... 3 hours later, you are still searching.

I found there is rarely an answer to that question. Sparker has a pre-requisites system. Any course can set another course as its pre-requisite! Now when you finish the "Intro to Scripting Games" course any courses that set "Intro to Scripting Games" as a pre-requisite show up as next courses you can take!

We also feature official course (well, not yet, but its coming).

Quality of learning Experience

If you've taken online college you'll notice this is an expecially bad problem. The teacher is hard to reach, and nothing he is teaching you makes sense!

I decided sparker would teach all its official courses to in-person focus groups (I spent 6 months testing a course on 5 students. I ended up throwing the course away because it was fundamentally flawed). I wanted our courses to have a unique standard of quality.

Each course on sparker alos features a builtin discord like chat! check it out. Now you can chat with other students, and get help directly from the teacher! Discord style code-highlighting is supported!

Live video and voice chat is coming as soon as I can get it working, or find and pay more poeple to help me build it.

Build a Portfolio...

Rather than a degree or certificate build a portfolio that you can use to showcase your skills.

A degree does not show employers what you are capable of. A portfolio of work you've done will. Thats why sparker doesn't offer certificates. Instead to "complete" a course you must make a certain number of showcase posts (exact number varies per course and is set by the course's author).

Free Courses

All beginner courses are free! Yes, literally!

Whats the point in paying for something if you don't know if its any good?
